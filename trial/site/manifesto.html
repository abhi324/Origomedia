Manifesto · 001

Growth
isn't random.
It starts from
the right origin.

§ 01

The industry kept scaling the wrong metric.

For a decade, creator marketing optimized for reach. Reach is the cheapest metric — it's what's left over after a platform's algorithm has done the hard part. It tells you who could have seen a thing. Not who felt anything when they did.

We think the interesting question is smaller, harder, and more useful: did someone save this? Did they send it to a friend? Did they remember it in a week? Those are the creator signals that actually compound into brand.

§ 02

Creators aren't vendors.

The word "influencer" flattened everything. A skincare dermatologist with 18,000 followers and a 20-year practice isn't the same thing as a college freshman doing get-ready-with-me videos. Both are valid. Neither is a vendor.

We run Origo like a creative agency of creatives — each person with a rate, a portfolio, a voice, and a seat at the table when briefs are written. If that sounds obvious, count how many agencies still don't do it.

§ 03

Smaller lists. Louder results.

Every Origo roster is deliberately under 150 creators at any given time. We turn down more applicants than we accept. Not because we're gatekeepers — because a small roster is what lets us know each creator well enough to match them to the right brand.

Big marketplaces confuse activity with progress. We'd rather do fewer campaigns that are unambiguously better than chase a vanity headcount on the homepage.

§ 04

Payments are a design choice.

How fast a business pays its creators tells you everything about how it sees them. 60-day terms say you'll wait. 90-day says we'll see. Net-15 says you're a professional and we know it.

Origo pays creators within 15 days of deliverable acceptance. Not as a feature — as an ethic. We pay a late fee if we miss it. The incentive is on us, not the creator chasing.

§ 05

Beauty is a serious category.

We chose skincare, beauty, and ritual on purpose. These are categories where trust is earned slowly, shown on the skin, and broken easily. The creators who do it well carry something close to medical responsibility — and they should be compensated and briefed like it.

We narrow the category so we can widen the craft.

This is the agency we wished existed when we started making things. So we built it.

— THE ORIGO TEAM · 2026
